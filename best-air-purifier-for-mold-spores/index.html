## Why HEPA Matters for Mold

Mold spores measure 1-30 microns. Most are 2-8 microns. True HEPA filters capture 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns and larger. That specification matters because “HEPA-like” or “HEPA-type” filters perform at 85-95% efficiency. The difference: 50 spores per cubic foot versus 500.

Pre-filters catch larger particles. Carbon filters absorb musty odors from mycotoxins. But the HEPA stage does the heavy lifting against spores.

## CADR Numbers Decode Air Changes

CADR measures cubic feet per minute of cleaned air. Higher CADR means faster room turnover. For mold control, you want 4-6 air changes per hour.

Calculate: Room volume (length × width × ceiling height) × 4 ÷ 60 = minimum CADR needed.

12×12×8 foot room = 1,152 cubic feet
1,152 × 4 ÷ 60 = 77 CFM minimum

## Filter Replacement Reality

HEPA filters don’t regenerate. They fill up, then airflow drops. Most manufacturers recommend 12-month replacement cycles. But mold environments accelerate clogging.

Visual inspection works better than calendar reminders. White filters turning gray or brown need replacement. Airflow reduction means efficiency loss.

## Placement Strategy

Position matters more than marketing admits. Mold grows in corners, behind furniture, near HVAC vents. Air purifiers work best in central locations with unobstructed airflow.

Don’t place units against walls. Three feet clearance on intake and exhaust sides. Avoid corners where dead air zones form. Keep away from humidity sources like humidifiers or aquariums.

Run continuously during mold season. Intermittent operation lets spore concentrations rebuild between cycles. The auto modes on modern units adjust fan speed based on particle detection.

## Advanced Features Worth Paying For

Air quality sensors separate premium units from basic models. The Coway includes particle, odor, and light sensors. Real-time feedback via colored LED indicators.

Timer functions save electricity. Sleep modes reduce noise and power draw overnight. Wi-Fi connectivity allows remote monitoring, but adds complexity and potential failure points.

UV-C lights claim antimicrobial benefits. Limited effectiveness in moving air applications. Ozone generators actively harmful. Stick with mechanical filtration.

## Professional vs. Consumer Units

Industrial air scrubbers move more air. Higher CADR ratings, larger HEPA filters, metal construction. The trade-off: noise levels unsuitable for occupied spaces.

The Alorair PureAiro HEPA Max 770 provides 550 CFM CADR for $400. Built for water damage restoration. Loud operation at 65 dB makes it basement-only equipment.

Consumer units balance performance with livability. The best mold air purifiers run quietly enough for bedrooms while moving sufficient air for spore control.

## Maintenance Schedule

Weekly: Check pre-filter, vacuum if washable or replace if disposable.
Monthly: Wipe exterior housing, check for unusual noises or odors.
Quarterly: Inspect HEPA filter condition through housing windows.
Annually: Replace HEPA and carbon filters regardless of appearance.

Skip the maintenance, lose the effectiveness. Clogged pre-filters reduce airflow by 30-50%. Dirty sensors give false readings.
