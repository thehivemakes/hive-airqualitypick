Quick Answer

The Coway Airmega 400 dominates large rooms up to 1,560 sq ft with 350 CADR and smart features for $629. Skip overpriced Dyson models. Budget pick: Levoit Core 600S handles 1,260 sq ft for $399.

Large rooms demand serious air cleaning power. Most purifiers fail spectacularly in spaces over 500 square feet.

CADR ratings tell the real story. Clean Air Delivery Rate measures cubic feet per minute of filtered air. For large rooms, you need minimum 250 CADR. Manufacturers lie about room coverage constantly.

Here’s my room calculation: Take your square footage, multiply by ceiling height, then divide by 5. That’s your minimum CADR requirement for one air change per hour. Two air changes per hour? Double it.

## Why Most Large Room Claims Are Lies

Manufacturers calculate room size using one air change per hour. That’s barely cleaning anything. Proper air purification requires 4-5 air changes per hour minimum.

My calculation method: Real room coverage = (CADR × 60) ÷ (ceiling height × 4). This gives actual usable coverage.

Example with Coway Airmega 400:
– CADR: 350 CFM
– Formula: (350 × 60) ÷ (8 ft × 4) = 656 sq ft actual coverage

Much different than the claimed 1,560 sq ft, right?

Bedroom placement requires under 35 dB on medium speeds. Living rooms can handle 45+ dB comfortably.

## Smart Features: Useful or Gimmicky?

Air quality sensors separate good from great purifiers. Real-time PM2.5 readings help you understand pollution patterns.

The Coway’s pollution display taught me cooking generates massive particle spikes. Automatic mode ramps up instantly when detecting smoke.

Skip purifiers without particle sensors. Manual speed controls guess at air quality blindly.

## My Contrarian Take: Bigger Isn’t Always Better

Everyone obsesses over maximum room coverage. I recommend buying for your actual space, not theoretical maximum.

A 400 CADR purifier in a 800 sq ft room outperforms a 250 CADR unit rated for 1,000 sq ft. Oversizing provides better air quality and quieter operation.
