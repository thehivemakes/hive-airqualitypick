## Why Your Asthma Needs More Than Just HEPA

Here’s the thing about asthma triggers: they’re not just dust mites and pollen floating around. You’ve got volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from cleaning products, pet dander that’s smaller than you think, and nitrogen dioxide from that gas stove. A 2018 study in Environmental Health Perspectives found that homes with HEPA + carbon filtration saw a 23% reduction in asthma symptoms compared to HEPA-only units.

That’s why you need dual filtration. HEPA catches the particles, carbon absorbs the gases and odors that can trigger attacks.

## Room Size Reality Check

Most people underestimate their room size. That “small bedroom” is probably 200+ square feet when you include the attached bathroom. Measure length × width and add 20% if you have high ceilings (9+ feet).

For severe asthma, I recommend sizing up. If your room is 300 square feet, get a unit rated for 400+ square feet. The air purifier can cycle the air more frequently on lower, quieter speeds while maintaining effectiveness.

## Filter Maintenance: The Hidden Costs

Here’s what manufacturers don’t tell you: pre-filters need cleaning every 2-4 weeks for optimal performance. Most units have washable pre-filters – use them. A clogged pre-filter forces the HEPA filter to work harder and reduces lifespan.

Set phone reminders. I’m serious. A dirty filter doesn’t just reduce performance – it can become a breeding ground for bacteria and mold. The opposite of what you want for asthma control.

The True HEPA and carbon filters typically last 6-12 months depending on air quality and usage. Don’t stretch filter life to save money. A saturated carbon filter stops absorbing VOCs and odors, while an overloaded HEPA filter loses efficiency rapidly.

## Installation and Placement Strategy

Position your air purifier at least 3 feet from walls and furniture for optimal airflow. Don’t hide it in a corner – you want unrestricted air circulation. For bedrooms, place it between your bed and the most common pollution source (usually the door or window).

Run it continuously on auto mode rather than cycling on and off. Asthma triggers build up gradually, and consistent filtration prevents accumulation. The energy cost difference between auto mode and off is minimal – maybe $2-3 monthly.

Keep doors closed when possible. An air purifier can’t effectively clean air that’s constantly being replaced by unfiltered air from other rooms.
