## Pillow and Bedding Protection

Pillows harbor 16 species of fungi after 18 months of use. SafeRest Pillow Protectors cost $13 each. Replace every 3-5 years versus pillows every 1-2 years at $40-80 each.

Down alternative fills shed fewer particles than goose down. Polyester fiber generates 40% fewer allergens than natural down. The Beckham Hotel Collection Pillows use gel fiber at $25 per pair.

Wash bedding weekly in 130°F water. Most home water heaters reach 120°F. Boost to 140°F or use hot sanitary cycle. Dust mites die at 131°F after 30 minutes.

## Air Purifier Calculations

Bedroom air purifiers seem logical but numbers tell different stories. A 300-square-foot bedroom needs 1200 CFM air changes for dust mite control. The Coway AP-1512HH delivers 246 CFM maximum. That’s 4.9 air changes per hour in a 300-square-foot room with 8-foot ceilings.

Air purifiers capture airborne particles. Dust mites live in mattresses and carpets. Address the source first: encasements and humidity control. Air purifiers provide supplemental benefit only.

## Implementation Timeline

Week 1: Install mattress and pillow encasements. Immediate 90% allergen reduction.
Week 2: Establish 45-50% humidity with dehumidifier. Dust mites begin dying.
Week 3: Begin twice-weekly HEPA vacuuming routine. Focus on mattress edges and carpeted areas.
Week 4: Wash all bedding in 130°F+ water. Replace old pillows.

Most people reverse this order. They buy air purifiers and special detergents first. Wrong approach. Containment beats capture every time.

## Common Mistakes and Overlooked Details

Zippered encasements fail at the zipper. Look for sealed zipper systems like SafeRest’s BugLock. Standard zippers create 2-inch gaps. The Protect-A-Bed AllerZip uses welded zipper construction at $89 versus $47 for SafeRest. Both work identically.

Vacuum bag disposal matters. HEPA bags trap allergens until disposal. Bagless canisters release particles during emptying. Cheap detail, significant impact.
