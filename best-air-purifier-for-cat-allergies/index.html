## Common Mistakes to Avoid

Placement affects efficiency more than specs suggest. Keep units 3 feet from walls, 6 feet from furniture. Corner placement reduces airflow by 30%.

Filter replacement timing matters. Waiting until filters look dirty reduces performance by 40%. Digital indicators prevent this mistake.

Running on low speed saves electricity but extends cleaning time. High speed for 2 hours outperforms low speed for 8 hours in removing initial allergen loads.

## The Contrarian Take

Everyone recommends placing air purifiers in bedrooms first. Wrong strategy for cat owners. Start with the room containing the litter box. Capturing allergens at the source prevents distribution throughout the home.

Second unit goes in your main living area where cats spend most time. Bedroom placement ranks third unless you allow cats on beds.

This approach costs more upfront but delivers better results than single-unit strategies.
